When Hurricane Maria struck Puerto Rico the morning of September 20th, it knocked the island into a total blackout. Since then, restoring electricity has been slow and scandalous for the three-plus million residents of the U.S. territory.

How long has Puerto Rico been without full power? As of Dec. 21, it’s 93 days and counting. When will power in Puerto Rico be restored? The Puerto Rican governor says full power will be restored by February, but the governor’s been wrong about this before.

More than half of Puerto Rico's power-making capacity has been restored, government officials say. That doesn’t necessarily mean more than half of Puerto Ricans have power — it’s just easier to measure power capacity than it is how many people have power.

In the aftermath of Hurricane Maria, more than 27,000 Puerto Ricans are believed to have come to New York, and NYC has been the hub of many Puerto Rico relief efforts.
