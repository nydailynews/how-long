The 2018 FIFA World Cup kicked off today in Russia with one notable absence — the U.S. Men’s team.

After losing to Trinidad and Tobago the World Cup-less Men’s team failed to qualify for the tournament for the first time since the Reagan Administration.

The U.S. Women’s team however is poised to defend its 2015 World Cup title next year in France (the tournament starts June 9, 2019), and will play to make sure the team qualifies (c’mon) this fall.

The women are three-time World Cup champions but received a paltry $2 million for the team’s 2015 victory, while 2014 Men’s champions, Germany, received $35 million.
